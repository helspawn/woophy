This time it was Woophy Rotterdam that had the difficult task of finding a winner. The task was especially complicated because ‘Landscape’ is a very Woophy like subject with many people submitting good pictures.<br />
<br />
Yet there were only 3 pictures which ended up on all of our shortlists and since two of the three pictures belonged to the same photographers it was clear who should be the winner of the contest. <br />
The only thing left was to choose the winning photo from his two entries. One picture was a very atmospheric, mysterious <a href="https://www.google.nl/search?q=turner&amp;hl=en&amp;prmd=imvns&amp;source=lnms&amp;tbm=isch&amp;ei=2k2xT42VG8Kl8gPnq8S3CQ&amp;sa=X&amp;oi=mode_link&amp;ct=mode&amp;cd=2&amp;ved=0CBcQ_AUoAQ&amp;biw=1599&amp;bih=827" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Turner</a>-esque photo of an island in the mist. The other was a wide mountain landscape in black and white with beautiful depth emphasized by the mist hanging between the hills. <br />
<br />
It was only because the latter of the two suited the theme of the contest a little bit more that we chose this one as the winner. <br />

If you still experience any troubles, missing images or have any remarks concerning the move to the cloud please mail support@woophy.com or post in this forum topic <a href="http://www.woophy.com/forum/2_5426_5.html" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">http://www.woophy.com/forum/2_5426_5.html</a></font></td></tr><tr><td style="padding-top:20px;padding-bottom:20px;padding-right:10px;padding-left:10px;border-bottom:1px dotted #66C029" valign="top"><p><font size="3" face="georgia,arial,helvetica,sans" color="#003900"><b>Sneak preview Woophy 3.0</b></font></p><font size="2" face="georgia,arial,helvetica,sans" color="#003900"><a href="http://www.woophy.com/photo/1315696" target="_blank"><p><img border="0" src="http://d2zrfkruk1fvbu.cloudfront.net/images/photos/193/97/m/1315696.jpg" alt="" title="" /></p></a>One of the big changes to come is the new Woophy map.<br />
<br />
A high-tech company from Cupertino CA decided not to allow Flash on their tablets and phones. So in order keep Woophy compatible with all devices we had to change the map technology. The technology we are going to use for the new map is provided by a another company from Mountain View CA , a company who happens to be a rival to the company not allowing the old map on their tablets.<br />
<br />
Or in language you understand we will make the Woophy map compatible with Apple devices by switching to Google maps soon.  In a joint effort between Woophy SF and Woophy Rotterdam we managed the switch to Google maps keeping the good things from the old map and adding the good things form Google. We really hope you will like the new map.<br />
